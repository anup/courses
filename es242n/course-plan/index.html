Course Plan

Course structure, student workflow, remaining post-midsem plan, quizzes, practice tracking, and grading policy.

INFO

Course information

Data Structures and Algorithms, ES 242(N). Instructor: Anup Kalbalia. Teaching Assistant: Priyanshi Agrawal. Original course-plan PDF, for reference only.

OVR

Course overview

This is a first course in data structures and algorithms. The course focuses on implementation, problem solving, and reasoning about efficiency.

PRE

Prerequisites and setup

Students are expected to know at least one programming language well. The course will use C. Students not comfortable with C should spend the first week catching up. Ubuntu is recommended because exams and quizzes will use the Ubuntu lab setup.

Completed Topics

Before Exam I, the course covered C fundamentals, arrays, pointers, dynamic memory, structures, strings, linked lists, time complexity, stacks, queues, and introductory recursion.

Remaining Course Plan

The plan below begins after Exam I. Monday quizzes are held before the new Monday topic.

8 Jun

Monday: Permutations and backtracking

Choice trees, recursive state, chosen[], used[], and the choose-recurse-undo pattern. Practice problems will be assigned after class.

10 Jun

Wednesday: No class

No class on 10 June. The substitute class is on Saturday, 13 June.

12 Jun

Friday: Backtracking problems

Subsets, generate parentheses, and more practice with the choose-recurse-undo pattern.

13 Jun

Saturday: Divide-and-conquer sorting

Merge sort and quick sort in one class: splitting, merging, partitioning around a pivot, and comparing the two recursive sorting patterns.

15 Jun

Monday: Quiz 1 and binary trees

Quiz 1: backtracking, merge sort, and quick sort. After the quiz: tree structure, recursive shape, and the start of binary tree traversals.

17 Jun

Wednesday: Binary trees

Traversals, height, size, leaf count, and recursive tree patterns.

19 Jun

Friday: Binary search trees

BST property, search, insert, min/max, inorder traversal, and deletion intuition.

22 Jun

Monday: Quiz 2 and heaps

Quiz 2: binary trees and BST basics. After the quiz: heaps, priority queues, array representation, insert, delete root, and heapify.

24 Jun

Wednesday: Graphs I

Graph vocabulary, adjacency matrix, adjacency list, and breadth-first search using a queue.

26 Jun

Friday: Graphs II

Depth-first search using recursion or an explicit stack, connected components, grid traversal, and BFS/DFS problem solving.

29 Jun

Monday: Hashing and review

Hash tables, collisions, chaining, open addressing, load factor, and selected review problems from the post-midsem portion of the course.

30 Jun

Tuesday: Final quiz

Cumulative post-midsem quiz covering backtracking, sorting, trees, heaps, graphs, and core implementation patterns. Hashing coverage in the final quiz will be light.

Grading Policy

The grading policy combines Exam I, post-midsem quizzes, CodeChef assignment contests, regular contest practice, diagnostic participation, and an attendance bonus.

Total

100 marks + up to 3 attendance bonus marks.

50

Exam I and post-midsem quizzes

Exam I counts for 25 marks. The post-midsem quizzes count for 25 marks: Quiz 1 and Quiz 2 count for 5 marks each; the final quiz counts for 15 marks.

32

Assignment contests on CodeChef

Seven CodeChef Starters will be considered. Best four performances count, 8 marks each. For START238 and START239 the scoring was: 4+ solved = 8, 3 solved = 7, 2 solved = 5, 1 solved = 3, participation with no solved problem = 1. From START240 onward the scale is: 5+ solved = 8, 4 solved = 7, 3 solved = 6, 2 solved = 5, 1 solved = 3, participation with no solved problem = 1. The first assignment Starter may use any language. From the second assignment Starter onward, only accepted C submissions count for assignment scoring; C++ submissions will not be rewarded for assignment marks. Scoring may be adjusted based on contest difficulty.

15

Regular practice

Participation on CodeChef, Codeforces, LeetCode, and AtCoder. Each eligible contest with at least one accepted submission earns 1 mark, capped at 15. A practice contest is C-eligible if at least one problem in that contest is solved in C; other problems in the same contest may be solved in any language. For grade calculation, contests are grouped by language, not by the date attempted: C-eligible contests are used for the required 12 practice marks. Non-C contests can contribute only to the remaining 3 practice marks, and only if the student has 12 C-eligible contests. A non-C contest attempted early is not lost, but it cannot replace any of the 12 required C-eligible contests. Counting window: 11 May - 30 June.

03

Diagnostic test

Beginning-of-course diagnostic test. Marks are awarded for attempting/attending the test, not performance.

+3

Attendance bonus

Attendance is outside the regular 100 marks. Students with at least 30% attendance are eligible; 30%, 60%, and 90% attendance correspond to 1, 2, and 3 bonus marks respectively. Applied only if it improves the final letter grade.
