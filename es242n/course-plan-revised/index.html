Course Plan — Revised

Revised course plan based on observed pace through Week 2 and the locked Exam I date. Each pre-exam week below shows the original plan alongside the revised version. The post-exam plan will be updated soon. A revised PDF with day-wise details — similar in style to the original course-plan PDF — will be available to download once finalized.

What changed

Three changes from the original plan:

  • Linear data structures (stacks, queues) moved into Week 3 so they are covered before Exam I. Original plan had time complexity here and stacks/queues spread across W2 and W3.
  • Recursion is split. A short intro is in Week 3 pre-exam; deeper recursion and sorting (merge sort, quicksort) move to post-exam.
  • Trees, heaps, and other non-linear structures move fully post-exam. Exam I is now linear data structures and foundations only; Exam II covers non-linear structures and graph algorithms.
INFO

Course information

Data Structures and Algorithms, ES 242(N). Instructor: Anup Kalbalia. Teaching Assistant: Priyanshi Agrawal. Download the original course-plan PDF.

OVR

Course overview

This is a first course in data structures and algorithms. The course focuses on implementation, problem solving, and reasoning about efficiency.

PRE

Prerequisites and setup

Students are expected to know at least one programming language well. The course will use C. Students not comfortable with C should spend the first week catching up. Ubuntu is recommended because exams and quizzes will use the Ubuntu lab setup.

Pre-exam plan — original vs revised

Each week shows the original plan followed by the revised plan. Topics only; day-wise details live on the per-class pages on the course home.

W1

May 11–15

Originally: C, arrays, memory, pointers, and structures.

Revised: Setup and Ubuntu install. Diagnostic test. C fundamentals and arrays.

W2

May 18–22

Originally: Time complexity and linear data structures.

Revised: Pointers and memory. Structures, unions, strings. Linked lists. Time complexity and stacks/queues move to W3 and W4.

W3

May 25–29

Originally: Recursion, searching, and sorting.

Revised:

  • Day 7 (25 May): One problem, three implementations — Josephus on array, pointer, and linked list. Big-O intuition through linear search, binary search, and bubble sort.
  • Day 8 (27 May): Recursion intro — base case, recursive case, factorial, sum, count linked-list nodes, recursive binary search.
  • Day 9 (29 May): Stacks and queues — built on linked-list mechanics, plus array-based versions and standard applications.

Sorting (merge sort, quicksort) moves post-exam.

Revised:

  • Day 10 (1 June): Stacks and queues problems — cumulative practice across pointers, linked lists, stacks, and queues.
  • Exam I: 3–5 June.

Trees and heaps move to W6.

Grading policy

Total: 100 marks, plus up to 3 attendance bonus marks. The grading structure is unchanged from the original plan. Download the original grading-policy PDF.

50

Exams

Exam I: 25 marks. Exam II: 25 marks.

32

Assignment contests on CodeChef

Six CodeChef Starters will be considered. Best four performances count, 8 marks each. Indicative scoring: 4+ solved = 8, 3 solved = 7, 2 solved = 5, 1 solved = 3, participation with no solved problem = 1. The first assignment Starter may use any language. From the second assignment Starter onward, only accepted C submissions count for assignment scoring; C++ submissions will not be rewarded for assignment marks. Scoring may be adjusted based on contest difficulty.

15

Regular practice

Participation on CodeChef, Codeforces, LeetCode, and AtCoder. Each eligible contest with at least one accepted submission earns 1 mark, capped at 15. A practice contest is C-eligible if at least one problem in that contest is solved in C; other problems in the same contest may be solved in any language. For grade calculation, contests are grouped by language, not by the date attempted: C-eligible contests are used for the required 12 practice marks. Non-C contests can contribute only to the remaining 3 practice marks, and only if the student has 12 C-eligible contests. A non-C contest attempted early is not lost, but it cannot replace any of the 12 required C-eligible contests. Counting window: 11 May – 30 June.

03

Diagnostic test

Beginning-of-course diagnostic test. Marks are awarded for attempting/attending the test, not performance.

+3

Attendance bonus

Attendance is outside the regular 100 marks. Students with at least 30% attendance are eligible; 30%, 60%, and 90% attendance correspond to 1, 2, and 3 bonus marks respectively. Applied only if it improves the final letter grade.
